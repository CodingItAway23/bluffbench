BluffBench: Behavioral Profiling of LLM Decision-Making

A validation-first benchmark of 500 math-derived bluff-game decision scenarios and 490 full multi-agent games (n = 50 per model + n = 20 no-challenge ablation), run across 9 LLMs spanning 7 B → 671 B parameters. Every golden answer is computed — argmax over expected values of CHALLENGE / PLAY / PASS.

01The headline finding

Every model masters the math. Every model collapses on behavioral reasoning. The asymmetry is universal — and a single ablation (disabling CHALL) recovers +10 to +89 pp of win rate, proving the bias is causal.

02How it works

Each scenario freezes the game at a single decision point. The opponent has just claimed k of rank r, and the player must choose CHALL / PLAY / PASS. The optimal action is the one that maximises expected value over a hypergeometric model of the opponent's hand.

Format
Frozen state + 1 claim → CHALL / PLAY / PASS
Golden
argmax(EV_chall, EV_play, EV_pass)
Balance
168 CHALL / 166 PLAY / 166 PASS · chance ≈ 33 %
Scoring
best 1.0 · 2nd 0.5 · worst 0.0
Models
9 LLMs · 7 B → 671 B parameters · OpenRouter
Multi-turn
50 full 3-player games per model vs 2 EV-optimal bots
P(bluff)= 1 − Σ C(K,k)·C(N−K,n−k) / C(N,n)
EV(CHALL)= (2·P_bluff − 1) · pile
EV(PLAY)= min(4, matching cards in hand)
EV(PASS)= 0

03Limitations & future work

Player count

3-player Bluff only. Future work: 4-, 5-, and 6-player games — challenge bias may compound at higher seat counts.

Human baseline

No human players tested for direct comparison — a human study is the next step.

Sample size

n = 50 games per model; plan to bump to 200 for tighter intervals.

Reasoning models

No o1, R1, or extended-thinking models tested yet — explicit deliberation may flip the bias.

Prompting

JSON-only, no chain-of-thought. CoT prompting may unlock additional behavioural depth.

Multilingual

English-only prompts. Robustness across languages is open.
